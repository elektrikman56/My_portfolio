Electronic Circuits & Systems Design Laboratory

Throughout an intensive academic semester, I successfully designed, simulated, and prototyped a series of over 10 specialized electronic projects. This laboratory series followed a rigorous engineering workflow: starting with Proteus simulations to validate circuit logic, followed by physical breadboarding and concluding with formal technical reporting. The series progressed from building essential laboratory tools to designing complex automated systems using analog and digital components.

Electronic Circuits & Systems Design Laboratory

The semester began with the construction of my own laboratory equipment, which served as the power and signal base for subsequent experiments:

  • Multi-Output Power Supply: Designed and assembled a regulated power supply capable of providing stable 5V, 9V, 12V, and 15V DC outputs.
  • Self-Made Signal Generator: Constructed a custom signal generator to produce various waveforms, used for testing and calibrating other designed circuits throughout the term.

Electronics Design Lab

The laboratory work covered a wide range of electronic principles and component integrations:

  • Automated Fire Alarm System: Developed a temperature-sensitive alarm using a 555 Timer IC and PTC/NTC thermistors. The circuit was engineered to trigger an audible alert based on specific thermal thresholds.
  • Operational Amplifier (Op-Amp) Applications: Implemented various Op-Amp configurations, including comparators, integrators, and differentiators, to process analog signals and manage sensor data.
  • Light-Sensitive Tracking (Final Project): The series culminated in an LDR-based solar tracking system (detailed in a separate section), which combined bridge circuits and differential sensing to achieve autonomous motion.
